The Red Planet's Evolution

Mars is one of the most explored bodies in our solar system, and it's the only planet where we've sent rovers to roam the alien landscape. NASA currently has two rovers (Curiosity and Perseverance), one lander (InSight), and one helicopter (Ingenuity) exploring the surface of Mars.

Billions of years ago, Mars was much warmer and wetter than it is today. Evidence points to a thick atmosphere and liquid water forming deep oceans, lakes, and winding rivers. The loss of its magnetic field caused the solar wind to strip away much of the atmosphere, leaving the frozen desert we observe today.
